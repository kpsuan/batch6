# Q29A-Q29C Image Generation Prompts for Gemini

This document contains all options from Q29A, Q29B, and Q29C (Section 6: End of Life Wishes, Batch 9) with their "How This Sounds" descriptions and corresponding Gemini image generation prompts.

## IMPORTANT: Diversity and Photography Requirements

**For ALL prompts below, append the following instructions:**

> "Shot on Kodak Portra 400 or Fujifilm Pro 400H film. Photorealistic, not illustration or cartoon. Real human subjects. Warm film grain, natural skin tones.
>
> **Rotate diversity across images:**
> - Ethnicities: Filipino, Indian, Nigerian, American, British, Chinese, Southeast Asian (Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian), Latino, Middle Eastern
> - **Occupations:** Vary widely — teachers, retired factory workers, shopkeepers, taxi drivers, tailors, accountants, grandparents, construction workers, bakers, hairdressers, librarians, farmers, engineers, cleaners, artists, truck drivers, small-business owners, social workers, chefs, translators. Healthcare workers appear only where the narrative explicitly requires one.
> - Include same-sex couples (two men or two women) in 20-30% of couple/family scenes
> - Include people with visible disabilities (wheelchair users, prosthetic limbs, mobility aids, hearing aids, visual impairment canes) in 15-20% of images
> - Vary ages, body types, and backgrounds"

## Q29A: Parting Words
**Question:** What have you always wanted to say to your loved ones?

**Question Title Image Prompt:** Create a suspended photorealistic photograph of a Filipino woman in her 50s, a small-business owner who runs a corner grocery, sitting at her kitchen table after closing up shop with a blank letter in front of her and a pen resting across it untouched. She has rehearsed what she wants to say to her mother a thousand times on the drive home. She has heard customers say things to their own families across oceans. But the page in front of her is empty because the words live in Tagalog and the page is in English and both languages fail at the same thing. Kitchen lamp light on a hand hovering over a page that has been waiting her whole life. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 film. Style: documentary photography, suspended, waiting, showing that asking what you have always wanted to say asks you to cross the distance between the words you carry and the page that has never held them.
