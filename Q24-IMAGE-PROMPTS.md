# Q24A-Q24B Image Generation Prompts for Gemini

This document contains all options from Q24A and Q24B (Section 5: End of Life Planning, Batch 7) with their "How This Sounds" descriptions and corresponding Gemini image generation prompts.

## IMPORTANT: Diversity and Photography Requirements

**For ALL prompts below, append the following instructions:**

> "Shot on Kodak Portra 400 or Fujifilm Pro 400H film. Photorealistic, not illustration or cartoon. Real human subjects. Warm film grain, natural skin tones.
>
> **Rotate diversity across images:**
> - Ethnicities: Filipino, Indian, Nigerian, American, British, Chinese, Southeast Asian (Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian), Latino, Middle Eastern
> - Include same-sex couples (two men or two women) in 20-30% of couple/family scenes
> - Include people with visible disabilities (wheelchair users, prosthetic limbs, mobility aids, hearing aids, visual impairment canes) in 15-20% of images
> - Vary ages, body types, and backgrounds"

## Q24A: Posthumous Legacy Narrative
**Question:** How do you want to be remembered?

**Question Title Image Prompt:** Create a reflective photorealistic photograph of a Jamaican woman in her 50s, a senior ward nurse, sitting alone in the hospital staff room at the end of a double shift, holding her name badge in one hand and a photograph of her family back in Kingston in the other. The question of how she wants to be remembered is not abstract -- it is geographic, professional, and personal at once. Her badge carries her destination-country identity. The photograph carries the one she left. Late evening staff room light on two identities held by the same hands. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 film. Style: documentary photography, reflective, bifurcated, showing that the question of how you want to be remembered is really the question of which version of yourself you trust to survive you.
