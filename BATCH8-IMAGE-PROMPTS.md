# Q28A-Q28C Image Generation Prompts for Gemini

This document contains all options from Q28A, Q28B, and Q28C (Section 6: End of Life Wishes, Batch 8) with their "How This Sounds" descriptions and corresponding Gemini image generation prompts.

## IMPORTANT: Diversity and Photography Requirements

**For ALL prompts below, append the following instructions:**

> "Shot on Kodak Portra 400 or Fujifilm Pro 400H film. Photorealistic, not illustration or cartoon. Real human subjects. Warm film grain, natural skin tones.
>
> **Rotate diversity across images:**
> - Ethnicities: Filipino, Indian, Nigerian, American, British, Chinese, Southeast Asian (Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian), Latino, Middle Eastern
> - Include same-sex couples (two men or two women) in 20-30% of couple/family scenes
> - Include people with visible disabilities (wheelchair users, prosthetic limbs, mobility aids, hearing aids, visual impairment canes) in 15-20% of images
> - Vary ages, body types, and backgrounds"

## Q28A: Being Valued
**Question:** What makes you feel seen, heard, and valued?

**Question Title Image Prompt:** Create a searching photorealistic photograph of a Nigerian woman in her 50s, a senior ward nurse, sitting alone in the staff break room after a double shift, holding her hospital ID badge in one hand and a photograph of her family back home in the other. The badge shows her professional title. The photograph shows her mother calling her by a childhood name the badge has never held. She has spent thirty years making sure other people feel seen. Nobody in this building has ever asked what makes her feel seen. The question is not clinical -- it is the gap between two names for the same person. Break room fluorescent light on a badge and a photograph that hold two different women. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 film. Style: documentary photography, searching, dual-identity, showing that asking what makes you feel valued requires naming which version of yourself needs the recognition.
