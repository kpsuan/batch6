# Q25-Q27 Image Generation Prompts for Gemini

This document contains all options from Q25, Q26, and Q27 (Section 5: End of Life Planning, Batch 7) with their "How This Sounds" descriptions and corresponding Gemini image generation prompts.

## IMPORTANT: Diversity and Photography Requirements

**For ALL prompts below, append the following instructions:**

> "Shot on Kodak Portra 400 or Fujifilm Pro 400H film. Photorealistic, not illustration or cartoon. Real human subjects. Warm film grain, natural skin tones.
>
> **Rotate diversity across images:**
> - Ethnicities: Filipino, Indian, Nigerian, American, British, Chinese, Southeast Asian (Vietnamese, Thai, Indonesian), Latino, Middle Eastern
> - Include same-sex couples (two men or two women) in 20-30% of couple/family scenes
> - Include people with visible disabilities (wheelchair users, prosthetic limbs, mobility aids, hearing aids, visual impairment canes) in 15-20% of images
> - Vary ages, body types, and backgrounds"

## Q25: Organ and Tissue Donation
**Question:** Do you want to donate your organs, tissues, and other body parts after you die?

**Question Title Image Prompt:** Create a contemplative photorealistic photograph of a Filipino woman in her 40s, a transplant coordinator nurse, sitting alone in the hospital chapel after her shift, holding a donor consent form she has helped hundreds of families complete. Today the form is blank and it is for her. Her hands know every line, every checkbox, every signature space -- but filling it out for herself requires crossing a distance no professional training covers. The gap between asking others to give and deciding to give yourself fills the chapel silence. Late afternoon chapel light through stained glass on a blank form held by experienced hands. Shot on Kodak Portra 400 film. Style: documentary photography, contemplative, reversal, showing that the question of organ donation lands differently when the person who asks others to give must decide whether to give themselves.
